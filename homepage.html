First Aid Guide

Bite wound

Step 1: Assess Your Pet
- Check for other injuries
- Look for signs of shock
- If concerned, cover wounds, keep warm, and go to the vet
Step 2: Assess the Bite Wound

- Bleeding heavily: apply pressure and go to the vet
- Skin missing: cover wound and go to the vet
Step 3: Flush the Wound
- For small, non-bleeding wounds, flush with lukewarm, salty water.

Burns

Step 1: Move to Safety
- Take your pet away from the burn source.
- Wash off chemicals with water if necessary.
Step 2: Cool the Burn
- Use cool water for 10-20 minutes.
- Avoid using ice or iced water.
Step 3: Seek Veterinary Care
- Look for symptoms like difficulty breathing.
- Call your vet for an emergency appointment.
- Follow their guidance for further¬†treatment.

Choking

Step 1: Assess the Situation
- Determine if your pet is choking or coughing.
- Check if they can breathe and if there's obstruction.
Step 2: Remove the Obstruction
- If conscious, use pliers or tweezers to remove the object
- If unconscious, sweep your fingers across the back of their throat to remove the object.
from their mouth.
Step 3: Seek Veterinary Care
- If unable to remove the object, call your vet for an emergency¬†appointment.
